High Rise Urbanism

2016
Graduation thesis at SPA, New Delhi

An attempt to use genetic algorithms in conjunction with climate modelling software to optimise architectural designs

Thesis Shmesis. Where's the Architectural plans?

Disclaimer: I'm not putting them up. Because this project turned out to be lesser about the plans, elevations and sections that you do in architecture school and more about a crash course in Procedural 3d modelling using Rhinoceros and a plethora of plugins from the Zoo. Plugins required: Grasshopper, Ladybird, Octopus, Galapagos, DiVA, ecoTect (I might be getting some of the names wrong)

Okay, no plans, then what?

This whole project took root in my mind back when we were trying our hand at solving urban design problems in a previous semester. I had just got my hands on the new Rhinoceros and was actively trying to learn procedural modelling in the grasshopper plugin.

The shear number of possibilities of modelling based on this new tool blew my mind to smithereens and I was voracioously consuming any grasshopper tutorials that I could lay my hands on.
