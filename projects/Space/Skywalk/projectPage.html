A reimagining of how pedestrian spaces should be built at above-ground level

Brief

This project was the result of a competition floated by the Delhi Government on a particularly vexing problem of creating overbridges that pedestrians would really use. The pilot project was to be constructed at the Pragati Maidan "W" crossing connecting foot traffic from four directions:

1. The metro station (what used to be Appu Ghar)
2. On the side of the Supreme Court of India
3. Lady Irwin College and Tilak Bridge Railway Station
4. The Hanuman Mandir side which connects directly to a large enclave of governement offices including the Income Tax Office.

Considerations

1. Structural Innovation: Design something new and unlike the pillar and post construction that is the norm.
2. Material Innovation: Use sustainable and modern materials to improve the aesthetic and longevity of the structure.
3. Safety: Ensure safety of women, elderly and children at all times.
4. Energy Efficiency: Provide systems for self-sustainability in terms of energy and water consuption.
5. PPP Viability: Enable revenue generation through viable PPP schemes.
6. Experience: Redefine the experience of taking a walk on an overbridge such that people are coaxed into actually using them.

Note:

This project is not explained in its entirety. All technical drawings and specifications have been withheld. Only artistic renderings are available.
