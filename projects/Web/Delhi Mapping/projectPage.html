delhi's disappearing heritage

2017

Classroom project at NID

Guides: Chakradhar Saswade & Amit Kapoor

A data visualisation of delhi's heritage sites as identified by INTACH

Brief


INTACH is short for The Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage is a non-profit charitable organisation registered under the Societies' Registration Act, 1860. The organisation aims to stimulate and spearhead heritage awareness and conservation in India. Over the years, INTACH has taken up restoration and protection of hundreds of monuments that fall outside the coverage of Archaeological Survey of India and other government agencies, and at times local authorities hand over the upkeep and restoration of heritage structures to INTACH directly.

Their work as INTACH's Delhi chapter is important as it maintains an exhaustive listing of some of Delhi's most important heritage structures whether they be listed as ASI protected monuments or even smaller privately owned structures of historical or architectural significance.

For this project, I aim to bring light to the massive repository of heritage data available to the public and to show its rise and potential fall if not given due importance in public discussion.

This data for this visualisation was originally available on the INTACH Delhi website. It has since been published in a book: "Delhi, The Built Heritage: A Listing"

Delhi, The Built Heritage: A Listing

Data Gathering

Most of the data for this visualisation was copied from the now defunct INTACH flash based website that listed all the data publically.

It was later augmented with data from the book itself for updating the dataset to its current configuration.

Furthermore, the following tasks needed to be done:

1. Find the actual latitude and longitude of the monuments from the static maps given out by INTACH. This was done using Google Earth's custom maps and then converted to lat long coordiantes that were fed into the database on excel.

2. Copy the data as is for each listing in a database format that could be used for further development. For this, Microsoft Excel was used to create a complete csv file that listed the entire dataset in a format that could easily be read by a programming language like Javascript.

3. Cross reference the images for each listing from the dataset with appropriate images that could be used to identify the monument being listed.

All in all, a datset containing ~1300 entries of heritage sites across Delhi was generated.
